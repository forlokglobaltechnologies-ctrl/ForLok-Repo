## 1\. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**eZway** is a next-generation, community-driven ride sharing platform built for India. Starting with an initial rollout across **Andhra Pradesh and Telangana**, eZway is designed to eventually serve every state in the country.

eZway is not a taxi aggregator. It is a **cost-splitting platform** - a digital solution that allows individuals who are already travelling on a route to share their vehicle and proportionally split the fuel cost with co-passengers. This keeps the platform outside the scope of commercial transport regulation while delivering immense value to daily commuters and vehicle owners alike.

Unlike conventional ride-hailing apps that deduct a large share from every transaction, eZway enables money to move **directly between the eZway Rider and eZway Passenger** - the platform adds no markup, no hidden charges. Only the actual travel expense is shared.

The platform will **launch across major district headquarters of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana**, covering both states simultaneously, before scaling nationally.

We humbly seek government support, particularly in obtaining: - Access to the **Government Aadhaar Verification API** for user identity authentication - Communication to the **Transport Departments of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana** to formally register our intent and operating model - Support to scale eZway across all districts of India

## 5\. OUR SOLUTION

eZway provides a **ride sharing platform** that addresses all the above challenges:

### 5.1 Ride Sharing (Core Service)

An eZway Rider already travelling from Point A to Point B lists their ride on eZway. eZway Passengers heading in the same direction can book a seat. The total fuel cost is split proportionally between all occupants. **No profit is made — only expenses are shared.**

### 5.2 Direct Cost Splitting

eZway does not add any platform markup to ride transactions. The amount agreed upon between the eZway Rider and eZway Passenger is what moves between them directly. The only fees that may apply are standard payment gateway processing fees charged by banks (which the platform cannot control). This makes eZway fundamentally different from any commercial ride-hailing platform.

## 8\. COST SPLITTING MODEL

### What Makes eZway Different

Most ride-hailing apps operate on a commission-based model where the platform takes a cut from every ride, significantly increasing the cost for passengers and reducing the payout to drivers.

eZway works differently:

| Platform         | Platform Deduction on Each Ride        |
| ---------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Ola              | 20–25%                                 |
| Uber             | 25–30%                                 |
| Rapido           | 15–20%                                 |
| **eZway**        | **No platform deduction — direct pay** |

### How eZway's Cost Splitting Works

eZway is fundamentally a **technology platform for cost-splitting**, not a taxi service. When an eZway Rider shares their ride, every rupee agreed upon goes directly to the eZway Rider. The passenger only pays their proportional share of the actual travel cost.

**Example:**
- An eZway Rider travelling from Vizianagaram to Visakhapatnam spends ₹400 on fuel
- With eZway, 3 eZway Passengers split this cost: ₹100 each
- The eZway Rider's own fuel cost drops from ₹400 to ₹100
- Each eZway Passenger pays only ₹100
- **eZway adds nothing on top of this amount**

### Payment Gateway Note

Standard bank/UPI payment processing fees (0.5–2%) are charged by financial institutions (Razorpay, UPI networks) — this is not collected by eZway and is outside our control.

### How eZway Sustains Its Model

- **Social Impact First** — Ride sharing adoption is our core priority, not platform revenue
- **Optional Premium Features** — Future optional add-ons (promoted listings, priority support) for operational sustainability
- **Government Partnerships** — Seeking public sector collaboration and grants
- **Volunteer-Driven Launch** — Founders are operating with minimal overhead in the initial phase

## 12\. LEGAL & REGULATORY POSITION

### 12.1 Our Legal Standing

**eZway is NOT a commercial transport aggregator.**

Under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 and subsequent amendments:
- A **contract carriage** or **taxi aggregator** is defined as a service where a vehicle is hired for commercial gain by the operator
- In eZway's model, **no commercial hire arrangement exists** — the eZway Rider is already travelling and simply splitting fuel costs with co-passengers
- The **money exchanged is not a fare** — it is a proportional share of the actual fuel expense

This model is analogous to **carpooling among colleagues or neighbours** — a practice universally recognised as legal and encouraged by governments worldwide.

### 12.2 Formal Communication to Transport Departments

We intend to send formal intimation letters to:

**The Commissioner, Transport Department, Government of Andhra Pradesh, Amaravati**
**The Commissioner, Transport Department, Government of Telangana, Hyderabad**

The letters will include:

_Subject: Intimation Regarding Operation of eZway — A Ride Cost Splitting Platform_

_Respected Commissioner,_

_We wish to formally intimate your department about the launch of **eZway**, a mobile application platform designed to facilitate voluntary ride sharing and fuel cost splitting among private vehicle owners and co-passengers._

_eZway is NOT a taxi aggregator or commercial transport service. Our platform enables:_
1. _Private vehicle owners who are already travelling on a route to list their journey_
2. _Other individuals going in the same direction to join the ride and proportionally share the fuel cost_
3. _No profit motive — only actual expenses (fuel cost) are split_
4. _No platform markup — eZway earns nothing from ride transactions_

_This model is consistent with the Government of India's thrust on reducing carbon emissions, promoting shared mobility, and reducing urban congestion as outlined in the National Urban Transport Policy._

## 13\. GOVERNMENT SUPPORT REQUEST - AADHAAR VERIFICATION API

### 13.1 Current Situation

Currently, eZway performs Aadhaar verification through **manual admin review** — admin staff visually verify uploaded Aadhaar images. This is:
- Time-consuming (24–48 hours per verification)
- Prone to human error
- Not scalable for thousands of users at launch

### 13.2 What We Need

We request access to the **Aadhaar Authentication/Verification API** provided by:
- **UIDAI (Unique Identification Authority of India)**
- **MeitY (Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology)**
- **DigiLocker API** (for document verification)

These APIs would allow us to:
1. Verify that the Aadhaar number entered by the user is valid
2. Confirm the name and date of birth match the provided details
3. Perform OTP-based authentication directly with UIDAI
4. Reduce verification time from 48 hours to under 5 minutes
5. Eliminate fake identity registrations completely

### 13.3 Why We Need Government Support

Access to official Aadhaar APIs requires:
- Approval from UIDAI / MeitY
- KYC service provider licence or partnership
- Significant financial investment for commercial API access (₹5–15 lakhs per annum)

As a platform operating with no ride transaction markup, serving as a public mobility utility, we cannot sustain commercial API pricing at the launch stage.

### 13.4 Alternative Government Integrations We Seek
